### A. Supplement / Protein Subscription ⭐ (Your Strongest Angle)

**Why it works for you:** You already ran Bulk Bar. You know the product, the customer, and the buying pattern. Protein is bought every 4–6 weeks like clockwork — pure repeat-purchase business.

**Market opportunity:**
- Sri Lanka's whey protein market is valued at **$15M+** and growing at **12% CAGR**
- Gym culture is rising fast — CrossFit boxes, fitness influencers, university sports
- Most buyers currently use Daraz.lk (slow delivery, no relationship) or go to Supplement Warehouse LK (physical store) — there is a gap for a **personal, fast, trusted supplier**

**Startup cost breakdown (under $500):**

| Item | Est. Cost |
|---|---|
| Initial stock (2–3 brands, 5–10 units each) | LKR 30,000–50,000 (~$100–165) |
| Packaging (bags, labels, stickers) | LKR 5,000 (~$17) |
| Instagram page setup (Canva branding) | Free |
| Delivery first month (self-deliver or Domex) | LKR 3,000–5,000 (~$10–17) |
| **Total** | **~$130–200** |

**How to source:**
- **India (best option):** Import from Indian supplement brands (MuscleBlaze, AS-IT-IS, BigMuscles) via IndiaMART or direct WhatsApp with distributors. India-to-Sri Lanka shipping is fast and relatively cheap.
- **Local distributors:** Contact wholesalers already importing into Sri Lanka — buy in small bulk, repackage into monthly subscription packs.
- **Repackaging model:** Buy 5kg bulk whey, portion into 1kg or 500g labelled pouches under your own brand name. This is legal if you label ingredients correctly.

**How to sell:**
- Instagram page + WhatsApp Business
- Monthly "Protein Pack" subscription: customer pays upfront for 3 months and gets a small discount
- PayHere for card payments (no setup fee, 2.5–3.9% fee, supports recurring billing)
- COD for new customers to build trust

**Repeat order mechanism:**
- Monthly auto-remind via WhatsApp
- "Refill Alert" — message customers when their pack is about to run out
- Loyalty punch card: 10th order free

**Biggest risk:** Import duties and customs delays on supplements. Mitigate by sourcing locally from existing importers and just rebranding.

### B. Chinese Wholesale Jeans — Redesign & Resell

**Why it works:** Sri Lanka's Instagram boutique scene is booming (Nils Store: 140K followers, Bayleee: 94K followers). Jeans with a custom twist — distressing, patch embroidery, painted hems — command 3–5x the wholesale price. You don't need to be a designer, just a curator with good taste.

**Startup cost breakdown:**

| Item | Est. Cost |
|---|---|
| Sample order: 5–10 pairs of jeans (via agent) | $80–150 |
| Freight forwarder / agent fee (1688 order) | $20–40 |
| Redesign supplies (bleach, sandpaper, patches, thread) | $15–25 |
| Tags, packaging, branded polybags | $15 |
| Photography (phone + natural light) | Free |
| **Total** | **~$130–230** |

**How to source:**
- **1688.com** is the cheapest Chinese B2B platform (owned by Alibaba). Prices are 2–4x lower than Alibaba. The catch: it's in Chinese and ships only within China.
  - **Solution:** Use a sourcing agent like **Supplyia**, **Leeline Sourcing**, or **FreeShoppingChina** — they buy from 1688 on your behalf and forward to Sri Lanka via DHL/EMS (3–7 day air delivery).
  - MOQ on 1688: as low as **1–5 pieces per style** — perfect for testing before scaling.
- **Alibaba.com** — English interface, slightly higher prices, but suppliers often ship internationally directly.
- **What to look for:** High-waist wide-leg jeans, barrel-leg denim, cargo-pocket styles — these are trending globally and haven't saturated the Sri Lanka market yet.

**How to redesign (low cost):**
- **Distressing:** Sandpaper, razor, bleach wash (costs almost nothing)
- **Embroidery patches:** Buy from local fabric stores in Pettah or Colombo markets
- **Painted details:** Fabric paint + stencils — minimal cost, high perceived value
- **Custom label:** Iron-on or sew-in tags with your brand name (~LKR 500 for 50 tags)

**How to sell:**
- Instagram: post styled outfit shots (not product-on-hanger)
- DM-to-order: customers DM size, pay via PayHere link or COD
- Facebook Marketplace Sri Lanka (free listings, massive reach)
- WhatsApp catalogue for repeat customers

**Repeat order mechanism:**
- Drop model: release 5–10 new pieces every 2–3 weeks ("drops sell out = urgency")
- Loyalty customers get first access to new drops via WhatsApp group
- Once a customer trusts your sizing/quality, they re-order every season

**Biggest risk:** Shipping delays from China (2–4 weeks for sea freight). Use air freight for small orders to keep turnaround fast. Also, returns on custom-altered jeans are hard — size clearly.

### C. Wedding Niche — High-Ticket, Always in Season

**Why it works:** In Sri Lanka, weddings happen year-round (no strict season). Every wedding needs multiple products across months of planning — engagement, bridal shower, bachelorette, wedding day, reception. That's 5–8 order touchpoints per single couple, plus all their friends getting married next.

**Market opportunity:**
- Global wedding wear market: **$82.4B in 2024 → $109.9B by 2030** (13.5% CAGR)
- Sri Lanka 2025 trends: fusion bridal sarees, modern drapes, eco-friendly décor, handcrafted accessories
- Sri Lankan couples are actively seeking personalized, modern alternatives to traditional suppliers

**Best low-cost wedding products (made-to-order, no stockpile needed):**

| Product | Startup Cost | Sell Price | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Custom bridal invitation sets (digital + print-ready) | Canva Pro ($13/mo) | LKR 3,000–8,000 per set | 80–90% |
| Bridesmaid gift boxes (curated: scrunchies, face mask, personalised note) | LKR 800–1,200 per box | LKR 2,500–4,000 | 60–70% |
| Bridal hair accessories (pins, combs, veil clips) | Wholesale Pettah LKR 200–400 | LKR 1,500–3,000 | 75% |
| Wedding favour boxes (small sweets, dried flowers, candle) | LKR 300–600 per unit | LKR 900–2,000 | 65% |
| Custom hanger photos (bride's name on wooden hanger) | LKR 400–600 | LKR 1,500–2,500 | 70% |

**How to sell:**
- Instagram: bridal aesthetic feed — soft tones, styled flat lays
- Partner with bridal photographers (they recommend you, you credit them)
- Facebook wedding groups (very active in Sri Lanka)
- Referral model: every bride who orders gets a referral discount card for her next friend's wedding

**Repeat order mechanism:**
- Referral chain: bridal networks are tight — one happy bride = 3–5 future brides
- Anniversary gifts, baby shower extensions once relationship is built
- Seasonal: Valentine's Day, Christmas gifting packages for the same customer base

**Biggest risk:** High customization = time-intensive. Set clear turnaround times (minimum 5–7 days) and don't over-customize cheaply. Price for your time.

### D. Daily Essentials Subscription Box

**Why it works:** Subscription boxes solve the "forget to reorder" problem. Sri Lanka has no dominant subscription box brand — it's essentially a blue ocean. The model works best when the product is something people consume regularly but find mildly annoying to keep reordering.

**Best niche options for Sri Lanka:**

| Box Concept | Monthly Price | Contents | Est. Cost to Fill |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein Refill Box | LKR 5,000–8,000 | 1kg protein, creatine sachet, shaker sticker | LKR 2,800–4,000 |
| Women's Wellness Box | LKR 2,500–4,000 | Period care, herbal tea, face mask, dark choc | LKR 1,200–1,800 |
| Home Cleaning Bundle | LKR 1,500–2,500 | Concentrated cleaners, sponges, microfiber cloth | LKR 700–1,000 |
| Sri Lankan Snack Box | LKR 1,500–3,000 | Local artisan snacks, murukku, kokis, treacle | LKR 700–1,200 |

**How to operate:**
- Monthly pre-payment via PayHere (which supports recurring billing natively)
- WhatsApp group for subscribers: builds community, reduces churn
- Source from local Pettah market, Keells, or specialty wholesalers
- Pack and ship once per month — batched fulfilment is very manageable solo

**How to sell:**
- 3-month subscription with slight discount vs. monthly
- "Gift a box" option for birthdays — acquisition through gifting
- Instagram Reels of unboxing (very high engagement)

**Biggest risk:** Churn. People forget to cancel but resent being charged. Be upfront about billing, make cancellation easy, and the trust you build will retain more customers than trying to trap them.

## 3. Delivery Alternatives to Uber Eats / PickMe

You don't need Uber Eats or PickMe. Here are your real options:

| Service | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Domex** (domex.lk) | Express island-wide courier | Reliable, professional, small business friendly |
| **Parcel LK** (parcel.lk) | Island-wide parcel delivery | ISO 9001 certified, good tracking |
| **Kapruka Logistics** | Colombo + outstation | Also lets small merchants sell through Kapruka platform |
| **Sri Lanka Postal Service** | Cheapest option | Slower but very affordable for non-urgent orders |
| **Pronto Lanka** | Longer distance deliveries | More cost-effective than PickMe Flash for outstation |
| **Your own delivery** | Colombo area, first 10–20 orders | Motorbike or tuk-tuk, builds personal relationship |
| **WhatsApp COD** | Any area, trust-first customers | Meet at a landmark or arrange drop-off, zero cost |

**COD (Cash on Delivery) strategy:**
- Offer COD to new customers — removes their risk completely
- Charge a small COD handling fee (LKR 100–200) to offset the cash risk
- Once a customer orders twice via COD, push them to PayHere for convenience

**Instagram → WhatsApp → Deliver flow:**
1. Customer finds you on Instagram
2. DMs you for price/size
3. You send PayHere payment link OR arrange COD
4. You pack and drop off at Domex/Pronto — they handle rest
5. Customer receives in 1–3 days

Zero need for Uber Eats, PickMe, or a delivery app.
